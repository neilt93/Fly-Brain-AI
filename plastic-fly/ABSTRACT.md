# Connectome-Constrained Sensorimotor Behaviors and Modality-Specific Motor Channels in Drosophila

The Drosophila melanogaster FlyWire connectome provides a neuron-level wiring diagram of 139,000 neurons and 54.5 million synapses, but whether connectome-structured dynamics can support meaningful sensorimotor transformations without learning or task-specific optimization remains unknown. We built a closed-loop system coupling a Brian2 leaky integrate-and-fire simulation of the full FlyWire connectome to a MuJoCo-based biomechanical fly body (FlyGym v1.2.1), with biologically identified sensory populations encoding body state and environmental stimuli into the brain, and descending neuron populations decoding motor commands back to the body. Using this system, we demonstrate three connectome-encoded behaviors that emerge from wiring alone: causal locomotion control (10/10 ablation tests, forward drive reduced 46% by targeted silencing with graded dose-response), olfactory valence discrimination (opposite turning for attractive vs aversive odors, 6/6 tests at n=10 seeds), and visually guided looming escape (contralateral turning with escape index 1.11, abolished 21-fold in shuffled connectome controls). Analysis of the descending neuron interface reveals that direct sensory-to-motor connectivity is highly modality-specific (Jaccard index 0.005-0.060 across six modalities including auditory, thermosensory, and hygrosensory), with convergence occurring through modality-specific interneuron pools that share zero intermediates between visual and olfactory pathways. Population-level analysis shows that the intact connectome activates 25x more condition-responsive descending neurons than a degree-matched shuffled control (102 vs 4), and sensory conditions are linearly decodable from the DN population at 2.2x chance level — structure that vanishes when wiring is randomized. Thermo-hygrosensory signals converge on a single DN type (DNb05, 2 neurons) whose ablation collapses thermo/hygro throughput by 93-100% while preserving other modalities (specificity 16.4x), demonstrating that labeled-line function can emerge from a minority input to a multimodal neuron. These results establish that the connectome's synaptic wiring diagram, with uniform biophysical parameters and no learning, carries sufficient functional information for directional, stimulus-specific sensorimotor transformations and structured population codes — a computational baseline against which the contributions of plasticity, neuromodulation, and experience can be measured.
